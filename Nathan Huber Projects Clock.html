Tri-Star: Senior Capstone Project

During my Junior & Senior Year of high school, I was in a career tech course called Tri-Star. During these two years, half of my day I was in a electronics lab learning the basics to more advanced concepts of electrical engineering. For my Senior Capstone Project I decided to do a clock. (Exciting right...) Well instead of using a micro controller like an arduino to keep the time I wanted to take a more challenging route. This clock has no programming at all used in it, and uses the 60Hz frequency from the wall to keep the time. The 12 stage binary ripple counter is counting in binary each time the clock input of the ripple counter receives a pulse. So every second the binary ripple counter is clocked 60 times in one second. Each time the output equals a binary 60, the outputs are then fed through an AND gate. The output of the AND gate would reset the counter and pulse the CLOCK input of the first decade counter.
